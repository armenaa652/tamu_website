My Contribution

My name is Adam Armendariz and I am a Computer Engineering major in my Junior year at Texas A&M. All of my life I have strived to get more involved into computer science. Coming from a small town with very limited learning options, I had no choice but to teach myself the fundamentals of programming. I progressed from small computational programs, to learning different libraries like pygame and p5.js to make my own games. Certain aspects of what I learned may seem trivial, but played a pivotal role in my ability to think algorithmically. Coming into my freshman year at A&M I joined a research team called SpaceCRAFT, which had the goal of creating VR software that could be used by researchers to commit tests in different physics environments other than Earth’s. I was brought on to the team as a software developer for the planetary scaling team. The sub-team was in charge of the procedural generation of planets that we did not have data for. My contribution to the team mainly consisted of analysis of the generated data for lighting and UV mapping purposes. During my time with SpaceCRAFT I learned how truly exciting and rewarding creating software like that can be. Having such an open-ended problem allowed for the entire team’s creativity to flourish. Aside from that, over the summer I worked for an engineering camp at my local college. In this camp, I teach middle school and high school students the fundamentals of engineering, problem solving and programming. It is such a rewarding feeling to give the students opportunities that I did not have growing up. The thing I am most excited about however is coming this upcoming summer. I have been accepted at an internship with a company called CACI. With them I will be conducting contract work at NASA to take part in its GATEWAY project, which will be the “gateway” to eventually traveling to Mars.
